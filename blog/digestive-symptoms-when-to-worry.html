Persistent Digestive Symptoms: When to See a Doctor

Occasional bloating or reflux is common, but symptoms lasting more than two to three weeks, unexplained weight loss, blood in stool, persistent abdominal pain, or difficulty swallowing all warrant a proper evaluation.

Chronic digestive conditions are frequently manageable once correctly diagnosed. Self-treating with over-the-counter remedies for weeks at a time can delay identifying something that needs a different approach.
