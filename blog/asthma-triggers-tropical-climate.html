Managing Asthma in a Tropical Climate

Common triggers include dust, pollen, smoke, cold air, respiratory infections, and strong odours — and in a tropical climate like Mauritius, humidity, mould, and dust mites are particularly common culprits.

Good long-term management combines a few things: taking your prescribed preventive inhaler daily as directed, reducing indoor humidity, washing bedding in hot water weekly to reduce dust mites, avoiding outdoor activity when pollen counts are high, and always keeping your rescue inhaler on hand. Regular check-ups let your treatment plan adjust as needed rather than staying static for years.
