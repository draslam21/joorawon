Neurological Warning Signs You Shouldn't Dismiss

Sudden severe headache unlike any before, new numbness or weakness on one side of the body, slurred speech, sudden vision changes, or repeated dizziness and fainting all deserve prompt medical attention — some of these can signal an emergency.

Less urgent but still worth assessing are recurring headaches, tingling in the hands or feet, or memory concerns that are affecting daily life. An internal medicine consultation can determine what's happening and whether further specialist referral is needed.
